Announcing HellaSus, a new dataset for commonsense NLI.

Questions like the above are trivial to humans, with over 95% accuracy, but current state-of-the-art NLP models built on pretraining struggle with under 48% accuracy. We achieved this through RLF (Real Life Flirting), a data collection paradigm where we stood inside a university pub for ten days, ate nothing but bar snacks, and wrote down every single pickup line we overheard.* The key insight is to simultaneously shift the wit and stupidity of the dataset examples towards a critical “Goldilocks” zone wherein an eavesdropping scientist named Goldilocks would definitely roll their eyes, yet the examples would be often misclassified by state-of-the-art models.

Our construction of HellaSus, and its resulting difficulty, sheds light on the inner workings of deep pretrained models. More broadly, it suggests a new path forward for NLP research, in which new benchmarks are made by bored researchers interested in AI for social meh.

* We did not do this.
